The name RISC-V was chosen to represent the fifth major RISC ISA design from UC Berkeley (RISC-I (Patterson & SÃ©quin, 1981), RISC-II (Katevenis et al., 1983), SOAR (Ungar et al., 1984), and SPUR (Lee et al., 1989) were the first four). We also pun on the use of the Roman numeral "V" to signify "variations" and "vectors", as support for a range of architecture research, including various data-parallel accelerators, is an explicit goal of the ISA design.
